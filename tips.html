Tips & Gear

Everything you need to hike safely and comfortably

Essential Gear

Never head out unprepared — here's what every hiker needs

👟

Trail Footwear

Invest in waterproof hiking boots with ankle support. Break them in before long trips to avoid blisters.

🎒

The Right Pack

Day hikes need 20–30L packs. For multi-day trips, go 50–65L. Fit the hip belt before buying.

💧

Hydration

Carry at least 2L of water per person. A water filter or purification tablets are essential for backcountry.

🗺️

Navigation

Download offline maps before you go. A physical topo map and compass are backup essentials.

🧥

Layering System

Base layer wicks moisture, mid layer insulates, outer shell blocks wind and rain. Always carry a rain jacket.

🩹

First Aid Kit

Blister pads, bandages, antiseptic, ibuprofen, and a mylar emergency blanket. Know how to use them.

🔦

Headlamp

Even on day hikes — delays happen. A headlamp with fresh batteries weighs almost nothing and can save you.

🍫

Trail Nutrition

High-calorie snacks: trail mix, energy bars, jerky, and dried fruit. Pack 300–400 calories per hour of hiking.

☀️

Sun Protection

SPF 30+ sunscreen, UV-blocking sunglasses, and a wide-brim hat. Altitude amplifies UV exposure significantly.

Safety Tips

Hike smart, hike safe

📢

Tell Someone

Always share your trail plan, expected return time, and emergency contacts with someone not on the trip.

⛅

Check the Weather

Mountain weather changes fast. Check forecasts the night before and morning of. Turn back if storms approach.

🐻

Wildlife Awareness

Store food in bear canisters in bear country. Make noise on the trail. Keep a safe 100-yard distance from wildlife.

⏰

Start Early

Begin hikes at sunrise to avoid afternoon storms on peaks and to have buffer time if things go wrong.
